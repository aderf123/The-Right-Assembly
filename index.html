About The Right Assembly

The Right Assembly is a nonprofit organization based in Westerville, Ohio, committed to promoting righteousness, moral values, and positive social change.

Who We Are

A duly registered multi-faith organization driven by the urgency to elevate moral values in our society.

Our Mission

To stand with what is right and inspire individuals, families, and institutions toward righteous living.

Our Belief

We believe the best form of religion is righteousness expressed through responsible actions.

Our Vision

To take the message of morality and upright living to dedicated souls across the world.

Programs & Activities

Our programs are thoughtfully designed to educate, uplift, and positively impact lives through moral guidance, community engagement, and charity.

  • Youth Development (Ages 11–25)

    Guiding young people with moral education and values for responsible living.

  • Education & Counseling

    Workshops, conferences, and counseling delivered both online and in person.

  • Charity & Support

    Scholarships, food items, groceries, and essential materials for those in need.

  • Rural & Global Impact

    Focused outreach to underserved rural communities in developing countries.

The RelTol Initiative

The RelTol initiative is a carefully thought out approach, to encourage the act of religious tolerance amongst peoples of different faiths.

Clashes and conflicts, generating from religious intolerance have caused so much deep tension and violence between sects in different parts of the world, which ordinarily would have been avoided through the application of plain accommodation, maturity and tolerance.

The essence of this initiative is to push for restraint towards hyper religiosity among all faiths, thereby reducing the dangers associated with it.

At the Right Assembly, we support the practice of interfaith assimilation, where inter religious relationship, association and interaction is welcome and encouraged.

The theory of the RelTol initiative was born out of the concept that religion is absolutely a personal thing, and should be addressed as such at all times.

It is ok to safeguard one’s religion passionately, while respecting and accepting the religious beliefs of others.
